OneDoc Patient App Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 18, 2025

Camera Permission Usage

The OneDoc Patient App requests camera access for the following healthcare-related purposes:

Profile Management

  • Taking profile photos for user identification and verification
  • Updating profile pictures for healthcare provider recognition

Document Management

  • Capturing photos of medical documents, prescriptions, and test results
  • Uploading prescription images for medication verification
  • Taking photos of ID documents for account verification and security
  • Documenting medical conditions or symptoms for healthcare consultation

Health Records (myHealthBook)

  • Photographing medical reports and lab results
  • Capturing images for comprehensive health record documentation
  • Creating visual health timeline for provider consultation

Data Storage and Security

  • All photos are stored securely in Firebase Storage with end-to-end encryption
  • Images are encrypted during transmission using industry-standard protocols
  • Users maintain full control and can delete uploaded photos at any time
  • No photos are shared with third parties without explicit user consent
  • Healthcare provider access is limited to consultation purposes only
  • All data complies with South African POPIA (Protection of Personal Information Act)

User Control and Rights

  • Camera access can be revoked at any time in device settings
  • Users can choose alternative upload methods for document submission
  • All photo uploads are optional unless specifically required for identity verification
  • Users can request deletion of all stored images
  • Access to uploaded content can be restricted to specific healthcare providers

Location Services

OneDoc uses location services for:

  • Finding nearby healthcare providers
  • Real-time tracking during healthcare appointments
  • Emergency service location sharing
  • Provider navigation to patient location

Data Retention

  • Profile photos: Retained until account deletion
  • Medical documents: Retained for legal compliance (7 years minimum)
  • Consultation images: Retained as part of health records
  • Temporary verification photos: Deleted after successful verification
